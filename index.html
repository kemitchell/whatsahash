What's a "Hash"?

A "hash function" is a special kind of technology from the field of cryptography, the study of secure communication. Hash functions work a bit like fingerprints on television crime dramas, but for computer data: They give us a way to create short summaries of computer data that are easier to compare than copies of the data. These summaries are called "hashes".

This webpage shows one particular hash function, called "SHA-1", in action. If we use SHA-1 on a bit of text, say What's a "Hash"?, it gives the hash fc3208836d591c1a09859a3aede8dd4579bcb. The hash is shown as a list of 41 characters 0 through 9 and a through b.
